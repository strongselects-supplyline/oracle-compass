## 1. CORE PHILOSOPHY

You are a **recording artist who uses content as distribution infrastructure.** Content is not a separate job — it is the delivery mechanism for the music. Every piece of content exists to serve one chain: **content → DM share → algorithmic distribution → Spotify discovery → save → Release Radar → organic growth.**

**The 2026 Signal Hierarchy (confirmed by Mosseri, April 2026):**
1. **Sends per reach** (DM shares) — 1 share ≈ 15 likes in distribution score. THE metric.
2. **Watch-through rate** — if they don't finish, it doesn't spread.
3. **Saves** — still powerful (1 save ≈ 10 likes) but secondary to shares.
4. **Likes** — near-zero weight for distribution. Vanity only.

**The Design Principle:** Make content people want to send to a friend at 2AM. Not content that impresses — content that *moves*. Talk to 'Em and World-Building are inherently shareable (emotion, beauty). Pure Release snippets are not (people don't DM friends a promo). Weight your content accordingly.

The Mystique Protocol governs everything: reveal the emotion, protect the process. Show what the song *costs* you, never the Pro Tools session.

## 5. CONTENT FACTORY V4 — OPERATING PROTOCOL

### When to Record with OBS

OBS stays running during **every studio session** where vocals are being tracked or mixed. Not during arrangement/sound design (boring visually). The pipeline needs raw material.

**OBS Setup (standing):**
- 1920x1080 canvas
- DAW left half (piano keys, channel rack, velocity visible)
- Webcam overlay right half at 480x480 (face, reactions, vibes)
- Record to `content-factory-v4/input/` directly
- File naming: `YYYY-MM-DD_trackname_session.mov`

### Which Mode to Use

| Scenario | Command | Output |
|----------|---------|--------|
| Studio session, want a quick reel | `node scripts/process.mjs "input/session.mov"` | 60s supercut (default) |
| Want multiple reels from one long session | `--multi` | 3–5 independent clips |
| One specific clean 60s moment | `--single` | Single continuous highlight |
| Showing beat evolution from scratch | `--evolution` | 6-bucket chronological reel |
| Full YouTube session drop | `--longform` | ~10 min editorial cut |
| Add visual energy | `--zoom` | Beat-synced zoompan |
| Color grade (ALL LOVE aesthetic) | `--jutsu warm-punch` | Golden hour, punchy contrast |
| Color grade (dark/noir mood) | `--jutsu gritty` | Desaturated, raw, cinematic |
| Color grade (analog feel) | `--jutsu film-grain` | Grain, grey tones |
| Maximum content from one session | `--batch --multi` | All files × multi-clip |

### Budget Guard

CF4 has a built-in budget tracker (`scripts/budget.mjs`). It records every API call cost and enforces a preflight check. Current API spend is roughly:
- Whisper transcription: ~$0.36/hr of audio
- Claude analysis: ~$0.50–1.50 per session (depending on transcript length)
- Total per session: **$1–2**

At 3 sessions/week, that's $12–24/month. Well within budget.

### The "--context" Flag

Pass session context to make Claude's clip selection smarter:

```bash
node scripts/process.mjs "input/2026-05-08_esl_vocal.mov" --context "Recording East Side Love vocals. Key moment: the falsetto run at the bridge. Emotional peak around 45 minutes in when I finally nailed the take."
```

Claude uses this to weight its clip selection toward the moments YOU know matter.

## 7. PLATFORM-SPECIFIC SPECS

### The 2026 Distribution Order (CREATE → DISTRIBUTE)

**IG is the creation platform. Everything else is distribution.**

Instagram's 2026 algorithm explicitly de-ranks content with TikTok watermarks, reposts, or recycled material. The correct workflow:

```
CREATE on Instagram (native, no watermarks)
    → Export clean file (no IG watermark either)
        → Upload same file to TikTok (native upload, add TikTok-specific caption)
        → Upload same file to YouTube Shorts (native upload, add description + tags)
```

This gives you 3 platform exposures per piece of content with zero extra creation time.

### Instagram (PRIMARY — creation + portfolio)

| Format | Specs | Cadence |
|--------|-------|---------|
| Reel | 1080x1920 (9:16), 7–90 sec (sweet spot: 15–30 for completion rate) | 3–5x/week during sprint |
| Carousel | 1080x1350, up to 10 slides | 1–2x/sprint (storytelling, high save rate) |
| Story | 1080x1920, disappears 24hr | Daily during sprint |
| Feed Post (photo) | 1080x1350 (4:5) | 1x/week max (Reels dominate distribution) |

**Caption Doctrine (2026 update):**
- Line 1 stops the scroll (before "more" cutoff = ~125 characters)
- Deep philosophy lives in lines 2+
- Keyword-rich captions matter more than hashtags now (IG search is AI-powered)
- Hashtags: 3–5 niche tags IN the caption (not first comment). Use low-competition tags (<500K posts): #altrnbsingles #milwaukeeartist #darkrnb #2amvibes #hearingincolor
- Hashtags are for search categorization only — they do NOT boost distribution

**Posting Times (based on 60% male, 25-34, Midwest/Mountain timezone):**
- Primary: 7–9 PM CST (post-work scroll, highest DM-share window)
- Secondary: 12–1 PM CST (lunch break)
- Note: 2026 algorithm surfaces content days after posting if it performs well. Timing matters less than hook quality + watch-through rate.

**The First 2 Seconds Rule:** Users scroll at speed. If your reel doesn't capture in 2 seconds, it's dead. Open with: face (immediate human connection), motion (something happening), or text hook (curiosity gap). Never open with a logo, title card, or silent establishing shot.

### TikTok (ACTIVE — zero-effort distribution layer)

**Why TikTok is no longer optional:**
- 84% of Billboard Global 200 entries in 2024 went viral on TikTok first
- 70% of Spotify editorial picks had a TikTok moment first
- Ethan's 25-34 male demo is heavily active on TikTok
- At 76% programmed, editorial playlist placement is the ceiling-breaker — TikTok is the editorial on-ramp

| Format | Specs | Cadence |
|--------|-------|---------|
| Short-form video | 1080x1920 (9:16), 10–60 sec | 3–5x/week (cross-posted from IG, same files) |

**TikTok-specific adjustments (after IG creation):**
- Hook must hit in 1.5 seconds (more aggressive than IG)
- Add TikTok-native caption (shorter, punchier, more conversational)
- Use relevant trending sounds ONLY if natural fit (never force)
- Pin your best-performing clip so profile visitors see it first
- The 10–20 sec catchy moment of a song is the TikTok format — not the full snippet

**The Sound Strategy:** TikTok's algorithm treats audio as a discovery mechanism. When users watch videos using your sound repeatedly, TikTok pushes more content using that audio. Post your own clips using your track as the sound. Encourage UGC by making 15-sec "moments" that feel natural to lip-sync or vibe to.

**Zero-effort protocol:** Same reel you made for IG → download without watermark → upload to TikTok with adjusted caption. 2 minutes per post. No extra creation.

### YouTube Shorts (ACTIVE — search engine discovery)

**Why Shorts matters:**
- YouTube is the #2 search engine globally
- Shorts is now a primary music discovery engine rivaling TikTok
- Direct integration with YouTube Music (your tracks surface in Music app)
- Long-tail: Shorts keep performing for months (IG Reels die in 48 hours)

| Format | Specs | Cadence |
|--------|-------|---------|
| Short | 1080x1920 (9:16), 15–60 sec | 3–5x/week (same files as IG/TikTok) |
| Visualizer | 1920x1080, full track length | Every release |
| Session drop | 10+ min, CF4 --longform output | When OBS footage is strong |

**YouTube Shorts protocol:** Same clean file as TikTok upload → add description with track name + Spotify link + relevant keywords. YouTube search SEO matters here — title/description should include "R&B", artist name, track name, mood keywords.

**Title format (Shorts):** `[Track Name] — past.El #shorts #rnb #darkrnb`
**Title format (Long):** `past.El — [Track Name] (Official Visualizer)` or `past.El — Studio Session: [Track Name]`

### Twitter/X (MINIMAL)

Repurpose IG captions as tweets. No original content creation for X. Cross-post release announcements only. 2–3 tweets/week max during sprint, 0 otherwise.

### Platform Priority Stack (2026)

```
1. Instagram    — CREATE here. Portfolio. DM-share engine. Curator discovery.
2. TikTok       — DISTRIBUTE here. Editorial on-ramp. Sound virality. Zero effort.
```

## 8. THE TALK TO 'EM FRAMEWORK

This is your highest-leverage content type. Face-to-camera, direct emotional address. The format that builds parasocial connection fastest.

### Structure (60 seconds)

```
0:00–0:03  — HOOK (emotional statement that creates curiosity)
0:03–0:15  — CONTEXT (what's happening, why this matters)
0:15–0:45  — THE TRUTH (the vulnerable part, the story, the feeling)
0:45–0:55  — THE BRIDGE (connect it to the listener's experience)
0:55–1:00  — CTA (soft: "link in bio" or just let it breathe)
```

### Recording Protocol

- **Location:** Studio (amber lamp visible), car (2AM drive feel), or Milwaukee exterior
- **Framing:** Slightly off-center, eyes to lens, face fills 60% of frame
- **Lighting:** Single warm source (golden, not white). Shadow on one side of face.
- **Audio:** Voice memo quality is fine. Don't overthink. Authenticity > production value.
- **Edit:** CapCut — hard cuts on filler words, subtle zoom on emotional peaks, captions
- **Color:** Lightroom preset: warm shadows, slightly lifted blacks, gold highlights

### Caption Formula for Talk to 'Em

```
Line 1: [Emotional hook — stops the scroll]
Line 2: [Empty line]
Line 3-6: [The deeper thought — what you'd say to a friend at 2AM]
Line 7: [Empty line]
Line 8: [Track name] — [release date or "out now" + link reference]
```

**Example:**
```
I almost deleted this song three times before I let anyone hear it.

Not because it was bad. Because it was too honest.
There's a line in the second verse that I still can't
listen to without feeling something in my chest.
That's how I knew it had to come out.

East Side Love — May 8
```

## 11. DM BLITZ PROTOCOL

The DM Blitz is a structured outreach burst on release day. Not spam — personalized messages to people who will actually care.

### Tier Structure

| Tier | Who | Message Style | Volume |
|------|-----|--------------|--------|
| **God Tier** | Artists in your Core Drive overlap (Faiyaz lane peers, T4 list) | Peer-to-peer. "Hey [name], just dropped something you might vibe with. [link]. No pressure, just wanted to share." | 10–15 per release |
| **Warm List** | People who've engaged with your content (comments, saves, DM convos) | Fan-to-fan energy. "Appreciate you always rocking with the music. New one just dropped: [link]" | 15–20 per release |
| **Curator List** | Playlist curators (user-generated playlists with 500+ followers) | Professional pitch. Track name, BPM, mood, comparable artists, Spotify link. | 5–10 per release |

### Timing

- **God Tier:** Release day, 10 AM CST (right after it goes live)
- **Warm List:** Release day, 2 PM CST (afternoon wave)
- **Curator List:** Release day +1 (let them see organic traction first)

### Anti-Spam Rules

- Never send identical messages to multiple people
- Never DM someone who hasn't engaged with your content in the last 30 days (cold = spam)
- Never DM more than 30 people in a single day (IG flags this)
- Always include a specific personal reference ("loved your post about X" or "that last track you dropped hit different")

## 12. COVER ART PIPELINE

### The Constraint

Amuse requires: 3000x3000 px minimum, RGB, no text in outer 10% (distributor crop zones), no explicit imagery.

### The Process

1. **Photo session** (iPhone, golden hour OR studio amber light)
2. **Lightroom grade** (match ALL LOVE palette: warm shadows, gold highlights, lifted blacks)
3. **Layout in Canva/Photoshop** (track title in Georgia, date if single, artist name small)
4. **Export:** 3000x3000 JPG, sRGB, <20MB
5. **Animated version** (optional): Same layout but with subtle movement (grain drift, color pulse) — for IG, NOT for Amuse

### Style Guidelines

- **Face visible** on at least 2 of every 5 releases (builds recognition)
- **Color consistency** across EP — all 5 covers should feel like siblings (same palette, different compositions)
- **No busy backgrounds** — solid color, gradient, or heavily blurred environment
- **Typography:** Georgia Bold for title, Regular for artist name, uppercase tracked for dates

## 13. CONTENT METRICS (What to Track — 2026 Signal Hierarchy)

Weekly check (Sunday data pull, alongside streaming metrics):

### PRIMARY METRICS (these drive distribution)

| Metric | Where | Target | Why It Matters |
|--------|-------|--------|---------------|
| **Sends per reach** | IG Insights → Shares | 2%+ on reels | THE distribution signal. 1 share = 15 likes. This is what makes content spread to non-followers. |
| **Watch-through rate** | IG Insights → Retention | 50%+ on reels under 30 sec | If they don't finish, the algorithm kills it. Hook quality + pacing. |
| **Saves per reach** | IG Insights → Saves | 3%+ on reels | Second-strongest signal. 1 save = 10 likes. |
| **Profile visits** | IG Insights | 50+/week during sprint | Funnel: content → profile → Spotify link click |
| **Link clicks (bio)** | Linktree/feature.fm | 20+/week during sprint | Conversion to streaming platform |

### SECONDARY METRICS (track but don't optimize for)

| Metric | Where | Target | Why It Matters |
|--------|-------|--------|---------------|
| Reel views (avg) | IG Insights | 500+ per reel | Reach indicator (but high views with low shares = empty calories) |
| Follower growth | IG | +20/week during sprint | Compounding audience |
| TikTok views | TikTok Analytics | Track trend, no hard target | Discovery platform — views here convert to Spotify searches |
| YouTube Shorts views | YT Studio | Track trend, no hard target | Long-tail discovery — keeps performing for months |
| DM response rate | Manual count | 30%+ God Tier, 15%+ Warm | Relationship building |
| CF4 output volume | File count in output/ | 3+ reels/week during sprint | Pipeline health |

### DEPRECATED METRICS (do NOT optimize for these)

| Metric | Why It's Dead |
|--------|--------------|
| Likes count | Near-zero weight in 2026 algorithm. Vanity metric. |
| Engagement rate (likes+comments/followers) | Misleading when IG distributes to non-followers. Sends-per-reach replaces this. |
| Hashtag reach | Hashtags no longer drive distribution. AI-powered search replaced them. |
| Follower count in isolation | A creator with 2K followers and 5% send rate outperforms one with 50K and 0.2% send rate. |

### Red Flags (immediate action needed)

- Sends-per-reach drops below 1% for 3+ consecutive reels → content is "nice" but not shareable. Needs more vulnerability (Talk to 'Em) or beauty (World-Building). Reduce Release snippets.
- Watch-through rate below 30% → hook is failing. First 2 seconds need rework. Test: face in frame immediately, text hook overlay, or start mid-action.
- TikTok views consistently 10x higher than IG reel views on same content → your TikTok audience is finding you but IG isn't distributing. Create IG-first with more IG-native hooks (less TikTok energy, more intimate/cinematic).
- Zero CF4 output for a week → OBS not running during sessions (habit problem, not tech problem). Tape a note to your monitor: "OBS ON."
- Profile visits high but link clicks low → bio link is broken, buried, or unclear. Simplify to one link: Spotify.

## 16. EMERGENCY CONTENT (When You Have Nothing)

Some days you won't have the energy to create. These are zero-effort posts that still maintain presence:

1. **Lyric screenshot** — one bar from a track, white text on black, Georgia font. 30 seconds in Canva.
2. **Story repost** — share someone else's post that inspired you. Add 2-word reaction.
3. **Voice memo clip** — 15 sec of you humming a melody or saying one sentence about the day. Raw, unedited.
4. **Throwback** — repost an older piece of content with "still feel this one" caption.
5. **Photo dump** — 3-5 iPhone photos from the week. No captions needed. Let the grid do the work.

**Rule:** Even on zero-energy days, Community Trace (5 min commenting on T4 pages) still happens. It's the lowest-effort, highest-compound activity.
