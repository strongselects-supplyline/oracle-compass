Nadi Shodhana (Alternate Nostril Breathing) — 5 min, 2x/day

When: Morning (after Jnana Mudra, before exercise/work) + Evening (before bed, part of wind-down).

Setup
Right hand: Vishnu Mudra. Fold index + middle finger to palm. Thumb controls right nostril, ring finger controls left. Left hand rests on knee in Jnana Mudra.
Standard pranayama hand position. Don't overthink it.
Cycle
Close right nostril (thumb). Inhale left (4 counts). Close left (ring). Exhale right (4 counts). Inhale right (4 counts). Close right (thumb). Exhale left (4 counts). That's one round. Do 10–12 rounds = ~5 min.
Balances the autonomic nervous system (sympathetic/parasympathetic). Direct serotonin baseline builder. Dr. K identifies this as a safe, universally recommended practice.
Progression
Phase 1: 4-count inhale/exhale. Phase 2+: Progress to 4-count inhale, 8-count exhale (extending the exhale deepens parasympathetic activation). Advanced: Add 4-count retention (Kumbhaka) between inhale and exhale — only when 4:8 ratio is effortless.
The extended exhale is where the magic is. Don't rush the progression. Weeks, not days.

Trataka (Candle Gazing) — 5–10 min, morning only

When: Full S-Tier mornings only (non-DoorDash days). After Nadi Shodhana, before exercise.

Phase sequencing: Not Phase 1. Add in Phase 2 when morning schedule has space. During Phase 1, the compressed ignition (Jnana Mudra + protein + Kill List + go) is correct.
Setup
Dark or dim room. Candle at arm's length, flame at eye level. Sit comfortably. Spine tall.
Candle at eye level prevents neck strain. Dim room maximizes the afterimage effect.
Phase 1
External gazing: Fix eyes on the flame tip without blinking. 30–90 seconds. Eyes will water — that's normal. When you can't hold it, close eyes gently.
Builds single-pointed concentration (dharana). The inability to blink IS the training — you're teaching the attention system to hold one point.
Phase 2
Internal gazing: Close eyes. Hold the afterimage of the flame in your mind's eye. It will drift and fade — bring it back to center. 2–5 min. When the afterimage fully dissolves, open eyes and repeat from Phase 1.
This is the DMN quieter. Dr. K's framework: Trataka directly addresses the hyperactive Default Mode Network that drives depression/anxiety/rumination. The afterimage gives the mind a single anchor — it cannot wander AND hold the image simultaneously.
Total
2–3 cycles of External → Internal = 5–10 min. Hard exit at 10 min. This is not meditation — it's a concentration drill with a definite endpoint.
Hard exit prevents the ADHD "I'll just do one more cycle" trap.
Do NOT progress to Yantra Trataka (Phase 3 in Dr. K's framework). That requires a guru. External and Internal Trataka are self-safe and self-sufficient for your needs.

Kapalabhati (Skull-Shining Breath) — 2 min, optional

When: Pre-studio on heavy production days. Energy boost without caffeine.

How: Sharp, forceful exhale through the nose (pump the belly in). Inhale is passive (belly relaxes out). Start at 30 pumps, rest 30 seconds. Repeat 2–3 rounds.

Caution: Not before vocal takes (it dries the throat). Not after 1 PM (energizing, can affect sleep). Not during acute stress or anxiety (it's activating, not calming — use Nadi Shodhana instead).

Dr. K safe-list confirmed: Surya Namaskar, Nadi Shodhana, Kapalabhati, Trataka — all safe for self-practice without a guru. Everything beyond these four (kundalini activation, advanced kumbhaka, bandha sequences) requires guided instruction.

Warm water + honey. One cup. Not hot, not cold. Room temp to warm. Sip throughout session.
Hydrates the vocal folds. Honey has mild anti-inflammatory properties. Cold water constricts. Hot water can irritate.

Releases the neck/jaw clamp chain. Alexander Technique principle: the head leads, the body follows. Free the neck first.

Semi-occluded vocal tract exercises are the single most efficient warm-up. They reduce subglottic pressure and create optimal fold vibration with minimal effort.
