## 2. GROWTH PHILOSOPHY

**Quality of follower > quantity of followers.**

A creator with 3,000 followers where 5% DM-share every reel outperforms one with 50,000 followers at 0.2% share rate. The 2026 algorithm rewards engagement depth, not follower count.

**The Compound Growth Model:**
```
Great content → DM shares → reaches non-followers → new followers who actually engage
    → more DM shares → more non-follower reach → accelerating growth
```

This is a flywheel, not a ladder. One viral moment (1 reel with 10%+ send rate) can trigger weeks of elevated distribution. The goal is to create the conditions where that moment can happen — consistently shareable content, not one-off stunts.

**The 1,000 True Fans Math (updated for 2026):**
- 1,000 people who save every release = guaranteed Release Radar placement
- 1,000 people who DM-share your reels = guaranteed Explore page distribution
- You need ~3,000 IG followers with 30% active engagement to hit these numbers
- Current: 2,200 followers, engagement suppressed by ratio. Target: 4,000 by Aug 1 with 3:1+ ratio.

## 3. THE THREE GROWTH ENGINES

### Engine 1: Organic Content (Department 2 → Department 3 handoff)

This is covered in the Content & Visual Playbook. The social growth plan's job is to COMPOUND what that playbook creates.

**What Department 2 provides:** 3–5 reels/week (Talk to 'Em, World-Building, Release), cross-posted to TikTok + Shorts.

**What Department 3 adds:**
- Strategic engagement in the first 30 minutes after posting (reply to every comment)
- Story engagement loops (polls, questions, countdowns)
- Community Trace (Pillar 5) executed as a growth tactic, not just a presence tactic
- Collaboration and cross-pollination with peer artists

### Engine 2: Community Trace (Outbound Engagement)

This is the most underrated growth lever for artists under 10K followers. It's free, it's fast, and it builds genuine relationships that convert.

**The Protocol:**

| Action | Time | Frequency | Where |
|--------|------|-----------|-------|
| **Comment on T4 artist posts** | 5 min | Daily | IG posts from artists in your Core Drive overlap (Faiyaz, Tiller, PND, 6LACK, Che Ecru, Chase Shakur, NO1-NOAH pages) |
| **Reply to comments on T4 posts** | 3 min | Daily | Find fans discussing the artist's music in comments. Leave a thoughtful reply. |
| **Like/comment on curator pages** | 3 min | 3x/week | User-generated playlist curators with 500–5K followers |
| **Respond to your own DMs/comments** | 5 min | Daily | EVERY comment gets a reply within 24 hours. Period. |

**Why this works in 2026:** Instagram's algorithm tracks your engagement patterns. Accounts that actively engage with others receive higher distribution scores. Additionally, leaving thoughtful comments on larger artist pages puts your verified badge in front of their audience. People click through when the comment is genuine and interesting.

**Comment Quality Rules:**
- Never: "Fire 🔥" or "Love this" (spam signals, gets buried)
- Always: Reference something SPECIFIC about the post ("the way that bridge hits at 2:03 is exactly why I kept this on repeat all week")
- Length: 10–25 words minimum. Long enough to demonstrate you actually consumed the content.
- Voice: Peer energy, not fan energy. You're a fellow artist in the lane, not a stan.

### Engine 3: Peer Collaboration & Cross-Pollination

**The Tactic:** Strategic relationship-building with artists at your level (1K–10K followers, same genre lane) for mutual content exposure.

| Collaboration Type | Effort | Reach Multiplier | When to Activate |
|-------------------|--------|------------------|-----------------|
| **IG Collab post** (joint-authored, appears on both profiles) | Low | 2x (both audiences see it) | Post-EP when you have a strong reel |
| **Duet/Stitch on TikTok** | Low | Variable (their audience + yours) | When a peer drops something you genuinely love |
| **Comment exchange** (organic, not coordinated) | Minimal | Low but compounds | Ongoing — just be present on each other's content |
| **Feature trade** (verse swap, remote collab) | High | High (shared release = shared audience) | Phase 2+ (post-Jul, when CREAM decision made) |
| **IG Live together** | Medium | Medium (real-time, but low replay value) | Only if natural — don't force |

**Who to target (from Core Drive + Gorilla Geo data):**

| Tier | Artists | Why |
|------|---------|-----|
| **Peer (same level)** | Chase Shakur, NO1-NOAH, Che Ecru, FRVRFRIDAY, Isaiah Falls, Ryan Trey, Avenoir | Same follower range, same lane, mutual benefit |
| **Reach-up (2-5x your size)** | Xavier Omär, bLAck pARty, Sabrina Claudio, Majid Jordan | Aspirational but accessible. One genuine relationship here = exponential exposure |

## 4. THE IG RATIO FIX (Gradual Unfollow Protocol)

**Target:** Move from 2,200/2,001 (1.1:1) to 4,000/800 (5:1) by October 2026.

**Why:** A 3:1–5:1 follower-to-following ratio signals to both the algorithm and human visitors that people follow you because of your content, not because you followed them first. This directly improves organic reach.

**Protocol (Sunday batches, 10 min max):**

| Week | Action | Target Following Count |
|------|--------|----------------------|
| Weeks 1–4 | Unfollow 50/week (accounts that haven't posted in 90+ days, brands, meme pages) | 2,001 → 1,800 |
| Weeks 5–8 | Unfollow 50/week (accounts that never engaged with your content) | 1,800 → 1,600 |
| Weeks 9–16 | Unfollow 30/week (non-mutual artists outside your lane) | 1,600 → 1,200 |
| Weeks 17–24 | Unfollow 20/week (final cleanup — keep only: peers, inspirations, real friends, active supporters) | 1,200 → 800 |

**Who to KEEP following (never unfollow):**
- Artists in your Core Drive overlap (peers and aspirational comps)
- Active supporters who engage with your content
- Real-life friends and family
- Curators and playlist owners
- Industry contacts (managers, A&R, producers)
- Inspirational accounts that fuel your creativity

**Anti-Flag Rules:**
- Never unfollow more than 50 accounts in a single day (IG flags this as bot behavior)
- Never unfollow + re-follow (manipulation signal)
- Sunday batch only — don't think about this during the week
- Use the native IG "Least Interacted With" and "Most Shown in Feed" sort in Following tab

## 5. TIKTOK ACTIVATION PLAN

**Starting from zero is actually an advantage.** TikTok's algorithm is the most meritocratic — it tests every piece of content against a small audience regardless of follower count. A 0-follower account can go viral on post #1 if the content performs.

### Setup (one-time, 15 min)

1. Create/claim @pastel.noir or @past.el (whichever is available)
2. Bio: "R&B from Milwaukee. EP out now. 🎶" + Spotify link
3. Profile photo: Same as IG (consistency for cross-platform recognition)
4. Link to Spotify (TikTok allows one link in bio)

### Content Strategy (zero extra creation)

All TikTok content is cross-posted from IG. The creation happens in the Content & Visual Playbook (Dept 2). TikTok distribution is Department 3's responsibility.

**Adjustments for TikTok (after IG-native creation):**
- Shorten caption (TikTok captions are less visible; hook lives in the video itself)
- Add 3–5 niche hashtags: #rnb #darkrnb #milwaukeemusic #newmusic #altrnb
- Use your OWN TRACK as the sound (this builds sound page plays — critical for discovery)
- Pin your best-performing video (profile visitors see this first)

### The Sound Strategy (TikTok-specific growth lever)

When you use your own song as the TikTok sound, every view on that video trains the algorithm to understand your audio. If it performs, TikTok will:
1. Push more content that uses your sound to users who liked similar sounds
2. Surface your sound in the "sounds" discovery tab
3. Enable other creators to use your sound (UGC = exponential reach)

**How to optimize for this:**
- Always use your track as the sound, not a trending sound (unless the trending sound IS your track)
- The 10–20 sec "moment" of the song should be the most catchy, lip-syncable, or vibe-able section
- Post multiple videos using the same sound (train the algo on that audio)
- When a video performs well, make a sequel/variation using the same sound

### Growth Targets (TikTok)

| Timeline | Followers | Avg Views | Strategy |
|----------|-----------|-----------|----------|
| Month 1 (May) | 0–100 | 200–500 | Cross-post everything, establish presence |
| Month 2 (Jun) | 100–500 | 500–2K | Sound strategy kicks in, algorithm learns your audio |
| Month 3 (Jul) | 500–2K | 1K–5K | If any video pops, triple down on that format |
| Month 6 (Oct) | 2K–5K | 2K–10K | Consistent presence, algorithm favors you |

**No-pressure rule:** TikTok is distribution, not creation. If cross-posting takes more than 2 minutes per video, something is wrong.

## 7. COMMUNITY BUILDING TACTICS

### The Warm DM System (Inbound)

When someone DMs you about your music (comments, questions, shares), respond within 24 hours with genuine engagement. This person is a potential super-fan.

**Response tiers:**

| DM Type | Response | Goal |
|---------|----------|------|
| "I love this song" | "Appreciate that more than you know. Which track?" | Convert into conversation → they become invested |
| "How do I get my music to sound like this?" | "Took a while to figure out. What are you working on?" | Peer relationship → future collab → mutual promotion |
| "You should check out my music" | Listen for 30 sec. If good: "That [specific element] is hard. Keep going." If not your lane: "Appreciate you sharing, keep building." | Genuine community member, not transactional |
| Story reply to your content | Quick acknowledgment + follow-up question | Keep the conversation going — IG rewards DM activity |

### The Super-Fan Identification System

Not all followers are equal. Identify and nurture your top 50 most engaged accounts.

**How to identify (monthly check):**
- Who comments on 3+ posts/week? (Visible in IG notification history)
- Who DMs you about releases? (Check DM history)
- Who shares your reels to their stories? (Check story mentions)
- Who pre-saves every release? (Check feature.fm analytics)

**How to nurture:**
- Reply to their comments FIRST (before others)
- Send them early access to snippets via DM (24 hrs before public post)
- Mention them in story reposts ("@username always comes through with the support")
- These 50 people are your street team. They don't need to be organized — just appreciated.

### The Close Friends List (IG Stories)

IG Close Friends creates exclusivity without effort.

**Who goes on Close Friends:**
- Top 50 super-fans (identified above)
- Peer artists you're building relationships with
- Curators who've engaged with you

**What goes on Close Friends stories:**
- Unfinished demos (15 sec, raw, vulnerable — "working on something. not sure about this one.")
- Behind-the-scenes that breaks the Mystique Protocol for this small group only
- Early announcements (24 hrs before public)
- Polls on creative decisions ("A or B version of this hook?")

**Why it works:** Exclusivity creates investment. People who feel like insiders become your most aggressive advocates. They DM-share your public content because they feel ownership over your success.

## 8. THE MEME ACCOUNT CONCEPT

**Status: CONCEPT ONLY — not activated until post-EP (June+)**

The premise: Run an anonymous meme/aesthetic account in the R&B/night-drive niche that builds audience in your exact target demo, then occasionally features your music as "discovered" content.

**Account concept:** @2amdriveplaylist or @latenight.rnb or similar
**Content:** Curated aesthetic posts (night city shots, moody quotes, R&B track recommendations, "songs for when you're in your feelings" carousel posts)
**Target:** Same 25-34 male demo that listens to your music
**Growth path:** Build to 5K–10K followers through niche content → occasionally feature past.El tracks as "recommendations" → funnel listeners who vibe with the aesthetic

**Why defer to June+:**
- Requires consistent posting (3x/week minimum) which competes with artist account during EP sprint
- Needs a backlog of 20+ posts before launch (batch-create during a low-energy week)
- The artist account is priority #1 until the EP compound window closes

**Anti-pattern to avoid:** Never make the meme account ONLY promote your music. It must genuinely serve the community (curate other artists too) or it reads as transparent and dies.

## 11. THE "DON'T DO THIS" LIST

| Tactic | Why It's Dead or Harmful |
|--------|------------------------|
| Follow-for-follow | Destroys ratio, attracts non-engaged followers, suppresses reach |
| Engagement pods (like-for-like groups) | IG detects coordinated activity, penalizes all members |
| Buying followers/likes | Immediate reach suppression. Also: fraud. |
| Posting 3x/day to "feed the algorithm" | Quality > quantity in 2026. 1 great reel > 3 mediocre ones. |
| Using 30 hashtags | Signals spam. 3–5 niche tags max. |
| Reposting TikTok content to IG with watermark | Explicitly de-ranked by Instagram's 2026 algorithm |
| Going viral through controversy/rage-bait | Wrong audience. You want the 2AM drive demographic, not Twitter arguers. |
| Automated DMs ("thanks for following!") | Spam signal. Damages trust immediately. |
| Posting without a hook in first 2 seconds | Algorithmic death — low watch-through kills distribution |
| Ignoring comments/DMs | Signals to algorithm that your audience isn't active. Signals to humans that you don't care. |
