## 2. Training program — Galpin 3-to-5 applied to your constraints

**Philosophy:** Minimum effective dose. You are an artist in a production sprint, not a competitive athlete. Training exists to protect the biological foundation — energy, testosterone, dopamine, posture, vocal health. The gym serves the studio.

**Structure:** 4 days/week. ~35-40 minutes per session including warmup and cooldown. Superset everything (Galpin-approved for non-elite: negligible strength loss, 3x time savings).

### Weekly template

| Day | Focus | Duration |
|-----|-------|----------|
| Monday | Strength A (push/pull upper + correctives) | 35 min |
| Tuesday | Cardio + mobility (zone 2 + hip/pelvic openers) | 30 min |
| Wednesday | OFF (studio marathon day) | — |
| Thursday | Strength B (lower body + correctives) | 35 min |

### Strength A — Monday (upper body, posture-corrective bias)

**Warmup (5 min):** Band pull-aparts × 15, wall slides × 10, cat-cow × 10, deep breathing × 5 breaths

**Working sets — superset pairs:**

| Pair | Exercise | Sets × Reps | Intensity | Rest | Why |
|------|----------|-------------|-----------|------|-----|
| A1 | Dumbbell bench press | 3 × 5 | 80-85% | — | Upper push. Dumbbells over barbell = shoulder-safe, full ROM. |
| A2 | Cable row (or dumbbell row) | 3 × 8 | 70-75% | 2-3 min after A2 | Upper pull. Prioritize squeeze at contraction. Corrects hunch. |
| B1 | Overhead press (seated, dumbbells) | 3 × 5 | 80-85% | — | Vertical push. Seated removes lower back compensation. |
| B2 | Lat pulldown (wide grip) | 3 × 8 | 70-75% | 2-3 min after B2 | Vertical pull. Activates lower traps (weak in Upper Cross Syndrome). |
| C1 | Face pulls (rope attachment) | 3 × 12-15 | Light | — | Posterior deltoid + external rotators. THE corrective exercise for caveman hunch. |
| C2 | Dead hang | 3 × 20-30 sec | Bodyweight | 1 min after C2 | Shoulder decompression + grip + lat stretch. |

**Cooldown (5 min):** Down-regulation breathing (see Section 5).

**Note on pull:push ratio:** 2:1 pulling to pushing volume is intentional. Upper Cross Syndrome means your pecs and front delts are dominant. More rows, pulldowns, and face pulls than pressing until posture normalizes (target: 6-8 weeks).

### Strength B — Thursday (lower body, pelvic floor aware)

**Warmup (5 min):** Goblet squat holds × 3 (30 sec each), glute bridges × 15, 90/90 hip switches × 8 per side, deep breathing × 5 breaths

**Working sets — superset pairs:**

| Pair | Exercise | Sets × Reps | Intensity | Rest | Why |
|------|----------|-------------|-----------|------|-----|
| A1 | Goblet squat (dumbbell or kettlebell) | 3 × 5 | Heavy | — | Full ROM squat. Goblet position enforces upright torso (anti-hunch). Stop at parallel — do NOT lock out hard (left knee Hoffa's). |
| A2 | Romanian deadlift (dumbbells) | 3 × 8 | Moderate | 2-3 min after A2 | Posterior chain: hamstrings + glutes + erectors. Counters anterior pelvic tilt. Hinge pattern. |
| B1 | Bulgarian split squat | 3 × 6/leg | Moderate | — | Single-leg strength + balance. Massive glute activation. Go to parallel, no lockout slam. |
| B2 | Hamstring curl (machine or Swiss ball) | 3 × 10 | Moderate | 2-3 min after B2 | Isolated hamstring. Weak hamstrings = pelvic floor compensation. |
| C1 | Pallof press (cable or band) | 3 × 10/side | Light | — | Anti-rotation core. Trains deep core stabilizers WITHOUT crunching (which worsens pelvic floor lock). |
| C2 | Hip flexor stretch (half-kneeling) | 3 × 30 sec/side | Bodyweight | 1 min after C2 | Tight hip flexors are the primary driver of anterior pelvic tilt + pelvic floor tension. |

**Cooldown (5 min):** Down-regulation breathing (see Section 5).

**Left knee rule:** All lower body exercises stop at parallel or just below. No heavy leg press. No plyometrics (yet). If any exercise causes anterior knee discomfort at the bottom of the range, reduce depth by 1-2 inches.

### Cardio days — Tuesday & Friday (zone 2 + mobility)

## 5. Down-regulation breathing — the 3-5 minute protocol

**Source:** Galpin/Huberman — "this completely changed the rate of recovery for me."

**When to use:**
- After every training session (mandatory — 3-5 minutes)
- After every DoorDash shift (1-3 minutes in the car before walking into studio)
- After every intense studio block (1 minute minimum)
- Before bed if still wired (5 minutes)
- After any high-volatility interaction (argument, rejection, bad news) — 1 minute minimum

**The protocol:**

1. Sit or lie down. Close your eyes.
2. Inhale through nose: 4 seconds
3. Exhale through mouth (or nose): 8 seconds (DOUBLE the inhale)
4. Repeat for 3-5 minutes minimum

**Alternative formats (all work — pick what feels right):**
- Box breathing: 4 sec inhale → 4 sec hold → 4 sec exhale → 4 sec hold
- Physiological sigh: double inhale through nose (short + long) → long exhale through mouth
- Simple nasal breathing: just breathe through nose only, extending exhale naturally

**What it does:** Manually shifts your nervous system from sympathetic (fight/flight/grind) to parasympathetic (rest/digest/create). Without this, the adrenaline from training or DoorDash crashes 3-4 hours later, killing your afternoon studio energy. Galpin: "I realized I was leaving workouts feeling great... then hitting a dip 3-4 hours later. The down-regulation fixed it completely."

## 6. Postural correction program — killing the caveman hunch

You have textbook Upper Cross Syndrome (tight pecs + upper traps, weak deep neck flexors + lower traps) combined with Lower Cross Syndrome indicators (pelvic floor lock + likely anterior pelvic tilt from studio chair hours).

**This is not cosmetic.** Your vocal tension chain (jaw → neck → shoulders) is directly downstream of your posture. Fix the posture, the vocal apparatus relaxes. The hunch is choking your instrument.

### Daily corrective stack (5 minutes — can be done in the studio between sessions)

| Exercise | Reps/Duration | What it fixes |
|----------|---------------|---------------|
| Chin tucks | 10 reps (hold 5 sec each) | Activates deep cervical flexors (weak in Upper Cross). Pulls head back over shoulders. |
| Band pull-aparts | 15 reps | Activates lower traps + rear delts (weak in Upper Cross). Immediate posture reset. |
| Pec doorway stretch | 30 sec per side | Lengthens tight pecs that are pulling shoulders forward. |
| Standing hip flexor stretch | 30 sec per side | Releases tight hip flexors driving anterior pelvic tilt. |
| Glute squeeze (standing) | 10 reps (hold 5 sec each) | Wakes up glutes that are sleeping from chair hours. Glutes counteract hip flexor dominance. |

**Do this stack:** Morning after DoorDash, mid-afternoon between studio blocks, evening if you've been in the chair for 3+ hours straight.

### Studio ergonomic rules

- Set a 45-minute timer. When it goes off, stand up, do 5 chin tucks and 5 band pull-aparts. Sit back down. This takes 60 seconds and prevents the hunch from deepening during long sessions.
- Monitor at eye level (not below — looking down reinforces forward head posture)
- Sit at front edge of chair occasionally to engage core (no back support = your muscles have to work)

### Timeline

Upper Cross Syndrome responds in 6-8 weeks of consistent corrective work. You should notice reduced neck tension within 2-3 weeks, which will directly improve vocal recording sessions.

## 8. Pelvic floor release protocol

Your pelvic floor is hypertonic (too tight), not weak. This is common in men with high stress, prolonged sitting, and somatized trauma. DO NOT do kegels — they will make it worse.

### Daily release work (2-3 minutes, can combine with mobility days)

| Exercise | Reps/Duration | Mechanism |
|----------|---------------|-----------|
| Diaphragmatic breathing (supine, knees bent) | 10 breaths | The diaphragm and pelvic floor move in tandem. Deep belly inhale = pelvic floor descends (releases). Long exhale = gentle engagement. This is the #1 release tool. |
| Happy baby pose | 60 sec | Opens hips and stretches pelvic floor from below. Gentle pull, don't force. |
| Deep squat hold (support as needed) | 60 sec | Full hip flexion lengthens pelvic floor. Hold onto something for balance if needed. |
| Child's pose with wide knees | 60 sec | Gravity-assisted pelvic floor release + hip opener. Breathe into lower back. |
| Adductor stretch (butterfly or frog) | 45 sec | Inner thigh tightness feeds into pelvic floor tension. Release the adductors, the floor follows. |

**When:** Morning after DoorDash (while doing down-regulation breathing) and/or before bed. These are passive positions — you can do them on autopilot while listening to a reference mix.
