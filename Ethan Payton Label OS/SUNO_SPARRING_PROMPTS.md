# SUNO SPARRING PROMPTS — Calibrated to Cyanite Data
*May 3, 2026 | Source: 21-track Cyanite analysis + BRAND_ARCHITECTURE_V3.md*

These are drop-in Suno prompts for generating one-shot "sparring demos" — beats to freestyle over, topline, and reproduce in FL Studio. Each maps to a proven sonic cluster in your catalog.

**How to use:** Paste prompt → generate → freestyle over it → if a melody or lyric lands, reproduce the beat from scratch in FL. The Suno output is the spark, not the product. Never release a Suno generation directly.

## USAGE NOTES

**Session structure:** Start with Prompt 1 (the core) to warm up in your natural register. If nothing lands after 2-3 takes, switch to Prompt 4 (slow burn) for a different energy. Prompts 2, 3, and 5 are for intentional exploration sessions when you want to build outside the core.

**Reproduction priority:** If a freestyle over a Suno generation produces a keeper melody or lyric, reproduce the BEAT from scratch in FL before recording the real vocal. The Suno generation is disposable. The topline idea is the asset.

**Suno settings:** Use "Instrumental" mode. Generate 2-minute clips. If the first 15 seconds don't hit, regenerate — don't wait for it to develop. You need the vibe in the intro to freestyle over.

**What NOT to do:** Don't try to match Suno's exact production in FL. The point is to capture the VIBE and the MELODIC IDEA, then build better production around it. Suno's mixes are compressed and AI-flavored — your FL production is the real product.
