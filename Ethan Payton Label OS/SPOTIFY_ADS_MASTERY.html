Auction vs. Reserved — Exactly What Each Is

✓ Use This Now
Auction Buying
~$18/day minimum · Dynamic CPM
You bid for impressions in real-time. Spotify's algorithm finds the right audience within your targeting for the best available price. Budget is flexible — pause, adjust, reallocate anytime. Come Back at $5K+
Reserved Buying
$5,000 lifetime minimum · Fixed CPM $10.00
Pre-book guaranteed impression inventory at a locked price. Delivery goal is impressions only — you're paying for eyeballs, not engagement. No performance optimization by the algorithm. Not the right tool for stream growth at any stage of your catalog.
The Decision Is Simple

You will never use Reserved. Even at $5,000 budget, Auction is superior for a music growth objective — the algorithm actively optimizes toward streams and engagement, while Reserved just passively delivers impressions at a flat rate. Reserved exists for brands running top-of-funnel campaigns. You are running a bottom-of-funnel stream acquisition campaign. Auction is the only answer at every budget level.

Part 3 — Do This First

Unlocking the "Streams" Delivery Goal — The Most Important Step

The "Streams" delivery goal being greyed out in your interface is the biggest thing to fix before launching. Here's exactly why it matters and how to unlock it:

Why "Clicks" ≠ "Streams"

Clicks optimization: Spotify's algorithm finds people most likely to click the ad — they go to your profile or track page, and may or may not listen. Many clicks result in 5-second bounces that don't generate a stream signal.

Streams optimization: Spotify's algorithm finds people most likely to actually stream the track for 30+ seconds — the full engagement signal the algorithm needs to push the song further. This is a fundamentally different optimization target. For music promotion, Streams is always superior.

How to Unlock Streams — Step by Step

1

In the Ad Set setup, find "Your content on Spotify"

At the top of the ad set targeting section, you'll see a toggle: Podcast · Music. Click Music.

2

Search for "Ethan Payton"

In the "Search for artists" field that appears below, type Ethan Payton and select your artist profile. You're declaring to Spotify that you own the content you're promoting and have the rights to advertise it.

3

Delivery goal will now unlock "Streams"

Once your artist content is linked, scroll to the Delivery section. "Streams" will now be selectable. Select Streams. This switches the algorithm's optimization target from click behavior to listening behavior — the correct signal for music growth.

4

Set Bid Strategy to Maximum Bid — start at the recommended range midpoint

Your account showed a recommended bid range of $8.27–$9.92 for audio. Start at the midpoint (~$9.00). Bidding at the middle-to-upper end of the recommended range improves delivery likelihood in the auction. You can lower this after 48 hours if spend is moving too fast.

What This Does to Your Algorithm

Once you're running Streams optimization, Spotify is feeding your campaign data directly back into the same algorithm that powers Discover Weekly and Radio. Every paid stream you generate through the campaign adds a data point to Spotify's model of "who listens to Ethan Payton." That model then influences organic recommendations — the paid campaign is seeding the organic algorithm. Every stream you buy via ads has compounding organic value beyond the immediate result.

Schedule — Lifetime vs. Daily Budget:

Your interface showed both options. Use Lifetime budget for a fixed campaign window (e.g., April 3–17). Spotify will automatically pace spend to avoid burning budget early. Daily budget is better for open-ended campaigns with no set end date. For a launch campaign tied to the April waterfall, Lifetime budget with a hard end date is the cleaner choice.
