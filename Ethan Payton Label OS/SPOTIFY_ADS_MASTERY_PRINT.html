Part 1

Auction vs. Reserved — Exactly What Each Is

✓ Use This — Always
Auction Buying
~$18/day minimum · Dynamic CPM
You bid for impressions in real-time. Spotify's algorithm finds the right audience within your targeting at the best available price. Adjust, pause, or reallocate anytime. Audio CPM: $8.43–$9.38. Every dollar optimized by the algorithm toward your delivery goal (Streams, once unlocked). The tool for all independent artist campaigns at every scale you'll operate at for the foreseeable future.
Not for Us — $5K+ Only
Reserved Buying
$5,000 lifetime minimum · Fixed CPM $10.00
Pre-book guaranteed impression inventory at a locked flat rate. Delivery goal is impressions only — you pay for eyeballs, not engagement. No algorithmic optimization. $10 CPM regardless of audience quality. Designed for brand campaigns with predictable quarterly budgets, not stream-growth campaigns. Don't touch this until budget is $5K+ and it makes strategic sense.
The Decision Is Simple

Even at $5,000 budget, Auction is superior for music growth — the algorithm actively optimizes toward streams and saves, while Reserved passively delivers flat impressions. Auction is the correct choice at every budget level for what we're trying to build.

Part 3 — Do This First

Unlocking the "Streams" Delivery Goal

Why "Clicks" ≠ "Streams" — Critical Distinction

Clicks optimization: Spotify finds people most likely to click the ad — they go to your profile or track page, and may or may not listen. Many clicks result in 5-second bounces that generate zero stream signal.

Streams optimization: Spotify finds people most likely to stream the track for 30+ seconds — the full engagement signal the algorithm needs to amplify the song. For music promotion, Streams is always the superior delivery goal. It's the one you need active before launching.

How to Unlock Streams — Step by Step

1

In Ad Set setup → "Your content on Spotify" → click Music

At the top of the ad set targeting section, find the Podcast · Music toggle. Select Music.

2

Search for "Ethan Payton" → select your artist profile

This declares to Spotify that you own the content you're promoting and have the rights to advertise it. Required before Streams unlocks.

3

Delivery section → select "Streams" (now available)

Once the artist content is linked, Streams becomes selectable. Select Streams. The algorithm now optimizes toward listening behavior, not click behavior.

4

Bid Strategy → Maximum Bid → set at ~$9.50

Your interface showed recommended range $8.27–$9.92. Start at the midpoint-to-upper end (~$9.50). Bidding nearer the upper end improves delivery likelihood while the algorithm learns your audience in the first 48 hours. You can reduce after 2 days if spend is moving too fast.

What This Does to Your Algorithm — The Compounding Effect

Streams optimization feeds your campaign data back into the same algorithm powering Discover Weekly and Radio. Every paid stream adds a listener's profile to Spotify's model of "who listens to Ethan Payton." That model influences organic recommendations for weeks after the campaign ends. Every stream you buy has compounding organic value beyond the immediate result.

Bid Strategy — Start at Upper-Middle of Recommended Range

Your interface showed recommended bid: $8.27–$9.92 with the note: "Bidding near the middle to upper end of the range often improves performance." Set maximum bid at $9.50 to start. After 48 hours: if hitting daily budget easily, reduce to $9.00. If delivery is slower than expected, bump to $9.92. The platform's own guidance is to bid nearer the upper end for better delivery in the first week of a new campaign.

Lifetime vs. Daily Budget:

Use Lifetime budget for a fixed campaign window (e.g., April 3–17) — Spotify paces spend automatically to avoid burning it all early. Use Daily budget for open-ended campaigns with no hard end date. For the April waterfall tied to specific drop dates, Lifetime budget with a hard end date is cleaner.
