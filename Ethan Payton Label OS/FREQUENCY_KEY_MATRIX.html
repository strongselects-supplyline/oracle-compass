Frequency × Key Matrix

For any song key and any target solfeggio frequency — instantly see what interval the frequency lands on, what emotional color that creates, and what sub-harmonic frequency to dial into your oscillator. The transposition problem solved as a lookup table.

How to Use This

Reading the Matrix

Step 1: Know your song key. Find that row in the matrix below.
Step 2: Find the column for the healing effect you want to embed (528 Hz Love, 396 Hz Liberation, etc.)
Step 3: The cell shows what interval that frequency lands on in your key and what emotional color it creates.
Step 4: Use the Sub-Harmonic Reference table to find the exact frequency (in Hz) to dial into your DAW sine oscillator.

The Core Rule

All intervals can work — they create different emotional textures. A Root match is the strongest resonance. A Perfect 5th is open and powerful. A minor 3rd is dark and emotional — often ideal for R&B. A tritone creates maximum dark tension — effective in intentional amounts. None are wrong. They're just different moods.

For a drone layer at -20 dB, even dissonant intervals become textural warmth rather than audible clashing. The ear won't identify the drone as a "wrong note" — it will just feel the harmonic field shift.

Sub-Harmonic Reference

Divide the target frequency down by octaves (÷2 each time) until you reach the sub-bass range your speakers and headphones can reproduce. Use the lowest practical value for headphone listeners; slightly higher for playback on bass-capable speakers.

DAW Setup — Step by Step

1. Create a new MIDI track. Insert any soft synth with a sine wave oscillator (Massive, Serum, Operator, ES2, etc.).
2. Set the oscillator to a single sine wave. Disable all envelopes — you want a sustained, pure tone.
3. Set the pitch to the "Recommended for DAW" frequency above (tune by ear using a spectrum analyzer, or enter the Hz directly in synths that support it).
4. Apply a tremolo/volume LFO on the track at the target brainwave frequency (6–8 Hz for Theta, 10–12 Hz for Alpha) to create the entrainment pulse.
5. Mix at -20 to -24 dB below the main mix. Apply a high-pass filter at 200 Hz on the master bus to prevent the drone from muddying the low end.
6. Check on headphones — the sub-harmonic layer is most effective in headphone listening.

Workflow

Per-Track Frequency Embedding Protocol

The 5-Step Process for Every Track

Step 1 — Write the song first. Never choose a key based on frequency targets. Songwriting authenticity comes first, always.

Step 2 — Identify the key. Major or minor, and its relative counterpart.

Step 3 — Consult the matrix. Find the row for your key. Identify which target frequencies land on Root, P5, P4, M3, or m3. These are your best-fit effects. Note which land on TT or m2 — use those sparingly if at all.

Step 4 — Choose your target effect(s). Match the effect to the song's emotional intention. A breakup song → Liberation (396 Hz) or Love/Repair (528 Hz). A song about trust or clarity → Grounding (174 Hz). A song about change in a relationship → Connection (639 Hz).

Step 5 — Embed the sub-harmonic layer. Use the Sub-Harmonic Reference table to dial in the exact Hz in your DAW. Set the LFO tremolo to 6–8 Hz for Theta entrainment. Mix at -20 to -24 dB.

Ethan Payton Catalog Notes

SEE ME / late-night R&B tracks: Target 528 Hz (Love/Repair) if key is near C, F, G, Ab, or A. For Ab minor or F# minor, the 528 Hz lands on a M3 or TT respectively — use 528's sub-harmonic at 66 Hz as a nearly inaudible bed, not as a drone layer. The entrainment effect (6–8 Hz Theta pulse) works in any key regardless.

Sweet Frustration / bridge singles: Target 639 Hz (Connection/Relationship) — specifically designed for music about wanting closeness. Best-fit keys: Eb, Ab, Bb, F#. If the track is in a different key, embed 80 Hz (Eb2 sub-harmonic) as an under-layer.

General principle: When the target frequency lands on m3 in the song key, the resulting harmonic texture is dark and emotionally charged — which is the core emotional register of past.El's catalog. A "dissonant" match by classical theory is often the most emotionally resonant choice for this genre.

Key Reminder

The Two-Mechanism Rule

This matrix covers absolute frequency embedding — where the solfeggio note is placed as a drone, pad layer, or sub-harmonic at the exact target frequency.

Brainwave entrainment (the 6–8 Hz Theta pulse, binaural stereo split, isochronic tremolo) operates completely independently of key and does not need this matrix. Those techniques work in any key because they function through pulse rate, not pitch. Apply them to every track regardless of key situation.

Interval mapping: standard Western chromatic scale · Solfeggio frequencies: traditional sources cross-referenced with Hz measurements · Sub-harmonic math: octave equivalence (÷2 per octave) · All frequency values to nearest 0.5 Hz
