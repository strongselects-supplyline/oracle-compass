**Goals:** Stage endurance, movement vocabulary, body control, nervous system regulation
**Constraint:** Max 30 min/day. Never competes with studio or DD time.
**Window:** 7:00–7:30 AM during sovereignty block, or immediately after.

## CALISTHENICS — MONDAY (Push/Core)

All bodyweight. No equipment needed. Hotel/apartment friendly.

### Warm-up (3 min)
- Arm circles: 20 each direction
- Shoulder dislocates (towel/band): 10
- Cat-cow stretch: 10 reps
- High knees in place: 30 sec

### Main Work (18 min) — 3 rounds, 45 sec rest between exercises
| Exercise | Reps | Progression |
|----------|------|-------------|
| Push-ups | 12–15 | → Diamond → Archer → Pseudo-planche |
| Pike push-ups | 8–10 | → Elevated pike → Wall HSPU negatives |
| Dips (chair/counter) | 8–12 | → Straight bar dips → Ring dips |
| Hollow body hold | 30 sec | → 45 sec → 60 sec |
| Plank to push-up | 8 each arm | → Add leg lift |
| Mountain climbers | 20 total | → Cross-body → Spiderman |

### Finisher (4 min)
- Max push-ups in 60 sec (record number, track weekly)
- Dead hang if bar available: 30 sec x 2

## CALISTHENICS — FRIDAY (Pull/Legs)

### Warm-up (3 min)
- Bodyweight squats: 15
- Hip circles: 10 each direction
- Lunge with twist: 5 each side
- Jumping jacks: 30 sec

### Main Work (18 min) — 3 rounds, 45 sec rest between exercises
| Exercise | Reps | Progression |
|----------|------|-------------|
| Bodyweight squats | 15–20 | → Bulgarian split squat → Pistol squat |
| Lunges (walking or static) | 10 each leg | → Jump lunges → Deficit lunges |
| Glute bridges | 15 | → Single-leg → Elevated |
| Inverted rows (table/bar) | 8–12 | → Feet elevated → Front lever prep |
| Pull-ups (if bar available) | 5–8 | → Negatives if needed → Weighted |
| Calf raises | 20 | → Single-leg → Weighted |

### Core Finisher (4 min)
- L-sit hold (floor or parallettes): 3 x 15 sec → build to 30 sec
- Side plank: 30 sec each side
- Flutter kicks: 30 sec

## RUNNING — TUESDAY & THURSDAY

### The Rules
1. **Pace:** Conversational. If you can't speak full sentences, slow down. Target: 11:30–12:30/mi to start.
2. **Duration:** 30 min continuous. Walk if you need to for the first 2 weeks. No shame.
3. **Route:** Doesn't matter. Flat is fine. Neighborhood, track, treadmill.
4. **Timing:** Before DD morning sprint. Run at 7 AM, shower, sovereignty stack, DD at 9.
5. **Breathing:** In through nose, out through mouth. If you can only mouth-breathe, you're going too fast.

### Progression
| Week | Goal |
|------|------|
| 1–2 | Walk/run intervals. Run 3 min, walk 2 min, repeat x6 = 30 min |
| 3–4 | Run 5 min, walk 1 min, repeat x5 = 30 min |
| 5–6 | Run 25 min continuous + 5 min walk cooldown |
| 7+ | Run 30 min continuous. Track pace — aim for sub-12:00/mi by week 8 |
| 12+ | Optional: one Tuesday or Thursday becomes 40–45 min (long run) |

### Running + Vocal Health
- Don't run and sing the same hour. Run first, let your breathing normalize for 30+ min before recording.
- Cold water post-run, not hot drinks. Hot constricts already-expanded airways.
- Mullein tea is AFTER the run settles, not during.

## DANCE — WEDNESDAY & SATURDAY

### Wednesday — Floor Work (20 min)
This is NOT choreography class. This is developing YOUR movement vocabulary.

1. **Pick one of your tracks** (rotate through the EP weekly)
2. **Play it 4 times:**
   - Listen 1: Stand still. Feel where your body wants to move. Notice the pockets.
   - Listen 2: Move freely. No mirror. Eyes closed if possible. Find what feels natural.
   - Listen 3: Mirror or phone camera on. Repeat what felt good from listen 2. Add intention.
   - Listen 4: Film it. Full run. This is your reference and potential content.
3. **Focus areas for R&B stage movement:**
   - Isolations: chest, shoulders, hips. Smooth, not sharp.
   - Weight transfer: how you shift from one foot to the other during a phrase.
   - Hand/arm vocabulary: what do your hands do during sustained notes?
   - Emotional anchors: what sections of the song demand stillness vs movement?

### Saturday — Performance Run (20–30 min)
1. Pick 2–3 songs from the setlist
2. Full performance: vocals (even half-voice), movement, transitions between songs
3. Film everything. This is stage rehearsal AND content factory material.
4. Watch the playback once. Note one thing to keep, one thing to change.

### Dance Study (passive — during DD or downtime)
- Watch: Chris Brown live performances, Usher, Miguel, SZA (for how she uses stillness)
- Watch: how they use the stage. Where they go. When they stop moving.
- The lesson isn't their choreography. It's their relationship between voice and body.

## NUTRITION — TRAINING DAY ADJUSTMENTS

Your existing fuel protocol (pre/mid/post session, protein at meals, caffeine cutoff) stays. These are the ADDITIONS based on training day:

### Run Days (Tue/Thu)
- **Pre-run:** Banana or toast with peanut butter, 20 min before. Light carb + some fat. Don't run fasted — you'll bonk by mile 2.
- **Post-run:** Add carbs to your breakfast. Oatmeal, rice, sweet potato. Your glycogen is depleted.
- **Hydration:** +16 oz water before the run, +16 oz after. On top of your normal gallon goal.
- **Protein timing doesn't change** — same protocol as always.

### Calisthenics Days (Mon/Fri)
- **Pre-workout:** Same as pre-session fuel. Eat 30 min before.
- **Post-workout:** This IS your pre-session meal on studio days. Prioritize protein: eggs, chicken, fish.
- **Creatine:** 5g daily with any meal (mix in water or coffee). Cheap, most evidence-backed supplement in existence. Helps with strength AND cognitive function. Take every day, not just training days.

### Dance Days (Wed/Sat)
- Same as normal fuel protocol. Dance sessions are moderate intensity — no special fueling needed.
- Stay hydrated during practice. Keep water in the room.

### General (Every Day)
- **Protein target:** 160g minimum (0.8g per lb at 205). You need this for recovery AND vocal cord tissue maintenance.
- **Key protein sources:** Chicken breast (31g/serving), eggs (6g each), fish (22g), Greek yogurt (15g), protein shake if short (25–30g).
- **Sleep:** 7+ hours non-negotiable. Everything else falls apart without this. Trataka + caffeine cutoff by 1 PM are your levers.
- **Mullein tea:** Keep daily. Lung capacity support for both running and singing.
- **Sauna:** Post-calisthenics days if available. Recovery + inflammation management.

## PHASE GATES

**Phase 1 (Weeks 1–4):** Survive. Build the habit. Don't chase intensity. If you skip a day, do the next one. Don't double up.

**Phase 2 (Weeks 5–8):** Lock form. Push-ups should be clean. Runs should be continuous. Dance sessions should have repeatable moves you can do without thinking.

**Phase 3 (Weeks 9–12):** Perform. String together 3-song performance runs with real movement. Film monthly to see progression. This is where you start looking like a performer who trains, not someone doing choreo.

**Phase 4 (Weeks 13+):** Stage-ready maintenance. The program doesn't get harder — it gets more automatic. Training serves the stage, not the other way around.
